Lakes

Western Washington offers a wide variety of lakes, from urban spots like Lake Union and Lake Washington to more remote alpine destinations such as Colchuck Lake and Lake Crescent. Popular options near the Seattle area include the accessible Lake Sammamish State Park and the wildlife-rich Anderson Lake State Park, while the Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest features beautiful spots like Watson Lakes.

Lakes Near Urban Areas

Lake Washington

The largest lake in the county, offering city views and activities like sailing and kayaking.

Lake Sammamish

Known for its state park which features swimming beaches, trails, and sports fields.

Lake Union

A popular spot for kayaking with views of the Space Needle and abundant wildlife like turtles.

Alpine and Mountain Lakes

Colchuck Lake

Famous for its iconic views of towering peaks.

Crescent Lake

A stunningly clear lake in the Olympic National Park, ideal for kayaking and hiking to Marymere Falls.

Watson Lakes

A pair of alpine lakes in the Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest that are great for day trips or overnight camping.

Snow Lake

A popular hiking destination that offers beautiful reflections and is perfect for a summer day trip.

Other Notable Lakes

Diablo Lake

Located in the North Cascades Mountains and known for its vibrant turquoise color.

Anderson Lake State Park

Features a freshwater marsh and diverse wildlife, accessible via hiking, biking, and equestrian trails.

Mayfield Lake

A clear and shallow lake with a waterfall, offering swimming and camping opportunities.
