National Parks

Mount Rainier National Park

Features stunning mountain views and diverse ecosystems.

Olympic National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its variety of ecosystems, including temperate rainforests, rugged coastline, and mountains.

North Cascades National Park

Offers impressive mountain scenery.

State Parks and Other Notable Areas

Deception Pass State Park

A popular spot with scenic views and opportunities for activities like canoeing.

Wallace Falls State Park

Features a multi-tiered waterfall and a network of trails for hiking and biking.

Dash Point State Park

Located on the Puget Sound with a large amount of saltwater shoreline, popular for water recreation and day-use.

Cape Disappointment State Park

Situated at the mouth of the Columbia River, offering historical sites and ocean views.

Saltwater State Park

Accessible from Interstate 5, this park features a beach, tide pools, and an underwater artificial reef for diving.

Fort Worden Historical State Park

A historic park on the Admiralty Inlet with gun batteries, museums, and beaches.

Saint Edward State Park

A large park on the shores of Lake Washington with beaches, forests, and trails.

Lake Wenatchee State Park

Known for its beautiful scenery and activities like horseback riding.

Lime Kiln Point State Park

A popular whale-watching spot on the San Juan Islands.

Palouse Falls State Park

A unique park featuring a stunning waterfall.
