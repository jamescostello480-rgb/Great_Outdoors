Popular Mountain Hikes Near Seattle

Mount Si
6.9 mi RT Elevation gain 3,150 ft Moderate

Classic Pacific Northwest hike — steep switchbacks, rewarding views of the Snoqualmie Valley and Seattle skyline on clear days.

Best time: Apr–Oct • Tips: start early, bring water, watch for snow late season.
Rattlesnake Ledge
4 mi RT Elevation gain 1,160 ft Easy–Moderate

Popular family-friendly hike above Rattlesnake Lake — short drive from Seattle, with dramatic cliff viewpoint and picnic spots.

Best time: year-round (snow possible in winter) • Tips: parking fills fast — carpool or take transit.
Mount Rainier National Park
Varies Giant volcano Strenuous options

A national treasure. Options range from short meadow walks to technical glacier routes. Plan ahead for elevation & weather.

Best time: Jul–Sep for lower trails • Tips: check park alerts & permit requirements for mountaineering.
Note: Trail conditions can change — always check official park pages before you go.

Safety & Preparation

  • Check the latest trail and weather reports. Mountain conditions can change quickly.
  • Pack layers — even summer hikes can get cold at elevation. Bring rain protection.
  • Carry plenty of water and snacks. Many Pacific Northwest trails are steep and exposed.
  • Tell someone your route and expected return time; cell service is unreliable in many areas.
  • Respect closures and private land. Stay on trail and leave no trace.

Flora & Fauna You May See

The area is home to Douglas firs, western hemlock, alpine wildflowers in summer, black bears (rare at lower elevations but present in the Cascades), marmots, and a wide variety of birds including gray jays and eagles. Respect wildlife by keeping distance and storing food properly.
