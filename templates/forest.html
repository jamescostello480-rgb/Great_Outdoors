Forests

Western Washington offers diverse forests, including the temperate rainforests of Olympic National Park (like the Hoh Rainforest), the extensive Mt. Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest with the Mount Baker Wilderness, and the Gifford Pinchot National Forest. For state-managed lands, visit areas like the Tahuya and Green Mountain State Forests for recreation or Tiger Mountain and Raging River State Forests.

National Parks and Forests

  • Olympic National Forests: Features lush temperate rainforests like the Hoh, Quinault, and Queets, known for their massive trees and mossy landscapes.
  • Mt. Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest: Stretches along the Cascades from the Canadian border to Mt. Rainier National Park and includes the Mount Baker Wilderness, offering rugged mountain and forest scenery.
  • Gifford Pinchot National Forest: Located south of Mt. Rainier, this is a popular area for recreation and overlanding.

State Forests

  • Tahuya and Green Mountain State Forests: Located in Mason and Kitsap counties, these are popular for off-road vehicles, hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.
  • Tiger Mountain and Raging River State Forests: Easily accessible areas for various forest activities.
  • Elbe Hills and Tahoma State Forests: Offer systems for off-road vehicles, horseback riding, and winter activities like cross-country skiing and snowshoeing at Tahoma State Forest.
  • Capitol State Forest: Another large state forest offering recreation opportunities.

Other Notable Areas

  • Hoh Rain Forest: A specific and iconic part of Olympic National Park, famous for the Hall of Mosses and Spruce Nature trails.
  • Grove of the Patriarchs: A unique grove of old-growth trees within Mount Rainier National Park.
  • Ginkgo Petrified Forest State Park: A unique park that showcases ancient petrified trees.
