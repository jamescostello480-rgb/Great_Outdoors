Ferries

You can visit many places via Western Washington State Ferries, including the San Juan Islands (from Anacortes) and the Olympic Peninsula (from Seattle, Edmonds, or Tacoma). Key routes from Seattle include trips to Bainbridge Island and Bremerton, while other major routes connect Mukilteo to Clinton and Port Townsend to Coupeville.

Major Ferry Routes and Destinations

From Seattle:

  • To Bainbridge Island: A popular, 30-minute trip to a nearby island with shops and restaurants.
  • To Bremerton: Another route to the Olympic Peninsula.
  • To the Port Orchard Ferry area.

From the Peninsula:

  • Port Townsend / Coupeville: Connects the northern Olympic Peninsula to Whidbey Island.
  • Edmonds / Kingston: Connects the mainland to the northern Kitsap Peninsula.
  • Mukilteo / Clinton: Connects the mainland to Whidbey Island.

From the Kitsap Peninsula:

  • Southworth / Fauntleroy: Connects the Kitsap Peninsula to West Seattle.
  • Vashon Island: Served by ferries from both Fauntleroy and Southworth.

From Tacoma:

  • Pt Defiance / Tahlequah: Connects Tacoma to Vashon Island.

To the Islands:

  • Anacortes / San Juan Islands: This route serves Lopez, Shaw, Orcas, and San Juan Islands. You can drive on or travel as a foot passenger.

Other Options

Private and Local Ferries:

  • Kitsap Fast Ferry: Passenger-only service from downtown Seattle to Bremerton, Kingston, and Southworth, Kitsap Transit.
  • Steilacoom Ferry: A vehicle ferry service connecting Steilacoom to Anderson and Ketron Islands.
  • Guemes Island Ferry: A vehicle and passenger ferry from Anacortes to Guemes Island.
  • Lummi Island Ferry: A passenger and vehicle ferry from Gooseberry Point to Lummi Island.

Plan Your Trip

  • Check schedules at wsdot.wa.gov/ferries
  • Bring binoculars for wildlife viewing
  • Pack layers for changing weather
  • Arrive 15 minutes early for foot passengers
