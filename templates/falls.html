Explore Washington's Beautiful Waterfalls

Waterfalls

Western Washington offers many waterfalls, from easily accessible attractions like Snoqualmie Falls to longer hikes leading to cascades such as Wallace Falls and Franklin Falls. The Olympic Peninsula has notable falls like Sol Duc Falls and Marymere Falls, while Mount Rainier National Park includes stunning options such as Comet Falls and Narada Falls. For a unique experience, check out the natural arches at Curly Creek Falls or the multiple tiers at Falls Creek Falls.

Near Seattle

  • Snoqualmie Falls: One of the state's most famous landmarks, with upper and lower observation decks, a gift shop, and the Salish Lodge.
  • Franklin Falls: Located near Snoqualmie Pass on I-90, accessible via a short trail.
  • Wallace Falls: Requires several miles of hiking to reach the series of waterfalls in the Central Cascades.
  • Twin Falls: Accessed from the Snoqualmie Valley.
  • Teneriffe Falls: A more challenging hike is needed for this waterfall.
  • Coal Creek Falls: A shorter hike off the Coal Creek Trail.

Olympic Peninsula

  • Sol Duc Falls: A very famous and unique waterfall that funnels into four different drainages, located in Olympic National Park.
  • Marymere Falls: A beautiful waterfall reached via a walk through old-growth trees.
  • Madison Falls: Another popular option on the peninsula.
  • Murhut Falls: Found on the north side of the peninsula.

Mount Rainier National Park

  • Comet Falls: One of the highest and most impressive waterfalls in the park, reached via a longer hike.
  • Narada Falls: A highly accessible waterfall with viewing platforms.
  • Christine Falls: A picturesque waterfall that can be seen right from the road.
  • Myrtle Falls: A popular and scenic waterfall within the park.

Southern Washington and Columbia River Gorge

  • Falls Creek Falls: Features three tiers and is surrounded by trees.
  • Panther Creek Falls: A beautiful waterfall located in the Gifford Pinchot National Forest near the Columbia River Gorge.
  • Lower Lewis River Falls: Part of the Lewis River, accessible via a 7-mile hike that includes multiple waterfalls.
  • Curly Creek Falls: Features a natural arch formed between its tiers.
  • Dougan Falls: The base of this waterfall has a natural swimming hole.
  • Loowit Falls: A stunning 185-foot fall near the toe of the Crater Glacier at Mount St. Helens.
