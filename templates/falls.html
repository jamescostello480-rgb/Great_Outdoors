Waterfalls

Western Washington is home to some of the most spectacular waterfalls in the Pacific Northwest. From easily accessible falls near Seattle to remote alpine cascades, there are waterfalls for every skill level and season. Popular destinations include Snoqualmie Falls, Wallace Falls, and the waterfalls of Olympic National Park.

Waterfalls Near Seattle

Snoqualmie Falls

One of the most iconic waterfalls in Washington at 268 feet tall. Located just 30 minutes from Seattle with easy viewing areas and a lodge at the top.

Wallace Falls

A scenic 7.2-mile roundtrip hike featuring a multi-tiered waterfall. Popular for hikers of all levels with excellent trail conditions.

Twin Falls

Located in the Snoqualmie Pass area, this short hike leads to a pair of beautiful cascading falls surrounded by old-growth forest.

Cascade Waterfalls

Nooksack Falls

A spectacular 90-foot waterfall located near Bellingham. Known for turquoise blue water and accessible via a 2-mile hike.

Bridal Veil Falls

A delicate, wispy waterfall located along Highway 2 near the town of Gold Bar. Easy to access with a short walk from the parking area.

Scenic Lakes Waterfall

Accessed via a longer hike in the Cascade Mountains, this waterfall offers spectacular alpine views and pristine backcountry experience.

Avalanche Lily Falls

A hidden gem waterfall in the Mount Rainier area, best visited in late summer when wildflowers are in bloom.

Olympic Peninsula Waterfalls

Marymere Falls

Located in Olympic National Park, this 90-foot waterfall is accessed via a scenic 1.8-mile loop trail through old-growth forest.

Sol Duc Falls

A beautiful waterfall in Sol Duc Valley featuring cascading water through a narrow gorge. Popular family-friendly hike at about 1.6 miles roundtrip.

Salmon Cascades

A series of cascades along the Elwha River offering scenic views and opportunities to spot salmon during spawning season.
